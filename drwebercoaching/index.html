Straight answers

Questions leaders ask before starting

Is this a real investment, and is it worth it?
Yes — this is senior-level executive coaching, not a course. The return isn’t a motivational high; it’s alignment, clearer decisions, and change that actually sticks. Most leaders find the cost of misalignment at the top far outweighs the investment in fixing it early.
I’m already stretched — how much time does it take?
The work is designed around a senior schedule. Because engagements are tailored — not pre-packaged — the cadence fits the leader and the organization, focusing only on what moves performance. It’s efficient by design.
Will this work for my industry and my team?
Leadership challenges — presence, alignment, communication, change — are consistent across sectors, which is why this work spans energy, pharma, finance, law, and technology. Every engagement starts with diagnosis, so it’s shaped to your context, not a template.
What if it doesn’t work for us?
That’s exactly why it opens with a diagnostic conversation rather than a commitment. If the work isn’t the right fit, you’ll know before you invest. Progress is measured in decisions and alignment you can see, not vague sentiment.
Why should I trust this over other coaching?
Two decades of work with leaders at IBM, Iberdrola, L’Oréal, UBS, Credit Suisse and Baker McKenzie — and coverage in Forbes, Entrepreneur and Business Insider. More importantly, this isn’t motivation or profiling; it’s structured coaching built for real organizational stakes.
Do I have to sign a long contract?
No. There are no pre-packaged programs and no pressure. The scope follows what the organization genuinely needs — a focused engagement or ongoing support — decided together after the diagnosis.
How quickly will we see results?
Clarity often shifts in the first sessions — how a leader communicates and where alignment is breaking down. Durable change in performance and organizational alignment builds from there. Addressing it early prevents larger execution and credibility problems later.

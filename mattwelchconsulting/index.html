Questions

Straight answers before we talk.

How much does it cost? +

Engagements are scoped to what you actually need — a one-off plan, a key-account push, or ongoing fractional leadership. You get a senior operator for a fraction of a full-time VP of Sales salary, with pricing agreed up front. No surprises.

I don't have time to manage another consultant. +

That's the point of a fractional leader — Matt takes work off your plate, not adds to it. He runs the distributor conversations and account pitches directly, and reports back with clear next steps rather than more meetings for you to run.

Does this work for my category? +

If you sell a consumer packaged good through distributors, retail, or venues, the mechanics are the same: structure the team, define the winnable markets, build the pitch, and manage the channel. The playbook adapts to your category and stage.

What if it doesn't work? +

You'll see sharper sales conversations and a clearer plan inside the first month. If the engagement isn't earning its keep early, you're not locked in — we agree the scope and you decide whether to continue.

Why not just hire a full-time sales leader? +

Eventually you might — and Matt will help you write the job description and structure the team when you do. But a full-time executive is a big, slow bet. Fractional leadership gets you the experience now, so you build the right team instead of the first team.

Are we locked into a long contract? +

No. Work is scoped by project or by month. Many brands start with a single planning or key-account engagement and expand from there once they see the difference. You stay in control of the commitment.

How fast will we see results? +

The plan and priorities land in the first weeks; the market execution follows on a 90-day arc. Distributor and retail cycles have their own timing, but you'll know exactly what's being worked and why from day one.

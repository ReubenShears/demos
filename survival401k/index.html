The problem

Your income is high. Your protection isn't.

If you're self-employed, the default system is built to tax you the hardest and shield you the least. Here's what it's quietly costing you.

Overpaying self-employment tax

Every 1099 dollar gets hit with the full 15.3% before income tax. Without a corporate wrapper, you're volunteering money to the IRS.

Retirement caps built for employees

A Traditional IRA maxes at $7,500. That's not a retirement plan for a real business — it's a rounding error on what you could shelter.

Money trapped on Wall Street

Standard plans lock you into their fund menu. You can't touch the real estate, metals, or private deals you actually understand.

Personal assets fully exposed

One lawsuit against you personally can reach your home and savings. With no LLC firewall, your business risk is your family's risk.

No liquidity when you need it

Traditional IRAs won't lend to you. Miss the loan provision and your own money is stuck behind a 10% early-withdrawal penalty.

Paperwork you don't have time for

DIY structures fall apart on compliance — Form 5500-EZ, plan documents, prohibited transactions. One wrong move and the shelter collapses.

Frequently asked questions

Is this actually worth it for what it costs?

The setup is a fraction of what most clients save in a single tax year. Sheltering $72,000+ and cutting self-employment tax through an S-Corp typically returns the cost many times over — before you count the investment upside of checkbook control.

How long does setup really take?

Most structures are built and funded in 1–2 weeks. You provide basic details about yourself and your business, choose your options, and make your initial contribution — we handle the plan documents, filings, and compliance.

Does this work for my situation as a 1099 / freelancer?

Yes. Solo 401(k)s are built for self-employed individuals with no full-time employees except a spouse — sole proprietors, independent contractors, freelancers, consultants, and single-owner businesses. Variable income is fine; contributions flex with what you earn.

What if it doesn't hold up with the IRS?

Every structure we build is IRS-compliant by design — proper plan documents, correct contribution mechanics, and Form 5500-EZ support once your plan assets pass $250,000. Compliance isn't an afterthought; it's the foundation of the whole architecture.

Can I roll over my existing retirement accounts?

Generally yes — old employer 401(k)s, Traditional IRAs, SEP IRAs, and Roth 401(k)s can roll into your Solo 401(k) (Roth IRAs are the exception). Consolidating makes your retirement far easier to manage and puts more capital under your checkbook control.

Am I locked into a long contract?

No. The structure we build is yours — a Solo 401(k), S-Corp, and LLC you own and control. There's no long lock-in; you keep a dedicated rep and compliance support for as long as you want the ongoing help.

Can I borrow from the plan if I need cash?

Yes. Solo 401(k) plans let you borrow up to 50% of the balance, capped at $50,000, typically repaid over five years. The interest you pay goes back into your own account — liquidity you simply don't get with a Traditional IRA.

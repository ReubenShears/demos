Straight answers

Frequently asked questions

What warranty do you provide on the buildings?+

A lifetime snow-load and wind-load certification, plus an industry-leading 365-day workmanship warranty — built to last for years, not seasons.

How much does a metal building cost?+

It depends on size, gauge, roof style, and options — which is exactly why we start with a free quote. Tell Mike what you’re building and he’ll give you an honest ballpark that fits your budget.

How long does it take to build a steel building?+

Erection time varies with size and complexity, but most projects run in the 4–8 week range from order to installed.

Can my metal building be customized?+

Absolutely. Size options, 17 color choices for roof, sides, and trim, door and window placement, and additional features — built exactly how you envision it.

Do I need a building permit?+

A permit is required in many localities and should be obtained before installation. Because our structures are certified, they’re engineered to pass the wind and snow requirements permit offices ask for.

What’s the difference between 14-gauge and 12-gauge framing?+

14-gauge framing is lighter and more cost-effective for most standard applications. 12-gauge is thicker and stronger, offering greater resistance to high winds and heavy snow. We’ll recommend the right one for your location.

What size concrete slab do I need?+

Your slab should be slightly larger than the building to seat the anchors — for example, a 31×41 slab for a 30×40 building. Thickness runs 4–6 inches, with 6 inches preferred for heavier loads like an RV.

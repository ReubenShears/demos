06 · Common questions

Answers before you ask

What does it actually cost?+

Validation is $995 and a full production build is $10K — a fraction of the six-figure quotes agencies hand out. On average founders save 72% versus a typical agency route, and you own everything at the end.

How can it possibly be done in 21 days?+

Because we validate first, keep the v1 focused on what customers actually need, and skip the agency overhead of layers, meetings and hand-offs. One senior builder shipping full-time gets more done in a month than a team gets done in a quarter.

Will this work for my niche?+

If you have a service you've delivered repeatedly, there's almost always a product hiding inside it. I've built for compliance, optometry, tradespeople, locksmiths and more — the validation sprint is designed to confirm the fit before you commit.

What if I'm not sure what to build?+

That's exactly what the $995 Prove-it sprint is for. You leave with a clickable first version, real user and competitor research, and a clear answer on what to build and why — before spending a penny on the full build.

What do I actually own at the end?+

Everything. The code, the product and the IP are yours outright — built on a standard, scalable stack you can host, extend or sell. No rented platforms, no lock-in, no white-label strings attached.

How is this different from hiring an agency?+

No account managers, no offshore hand-offs, no scope games. You work directly with one senior builder who's shipped for the likes of Microsoft and Google — faster, cheaper, and with skin in the game like a co-founder.

What if it doesn't work out?+

We start small on purpose. The low-cost validation step means you only commit to the full build once you know it's the right thing. And the ongoing partnership is month-to-month — you can cancel any time it stops being useful.
